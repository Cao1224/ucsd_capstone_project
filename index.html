Data

Dataset Overview

Our analysis utilizes the New York State Pretrial Release Dataset from 2023, comprising over 285,000 cases. This comprehensive dataset includes demographic information, criminal history, court proceedings, and pretrial outcomes, offering valuable insights into the pretrial release system in New York State.

Rearrest Outcomes

Analysis of pretrial outcomes shows:

  • 83.7% - No rearrest during pretrial period
  • 8.1% - Misdemeanor rearrest
  • 6.0% - Non-violent felony rearrest
  • 2.2% - Violent felony rearrest

Demographic Distribution

The dataset reveals important demographic patterns:

  • Age: Majority of cases involve individuals aged 20-39
  • Gender: Significant gender disparity in arrest rates
  • Race/Ethnicity: Notable variations in pretrial outcomes across different groups

Geographic Distribution

Cases are distributed across New York State's judicial districts, with significant concentrations in:

  • New York City boroughs
  • Major metropolitan areas
  • Regional variations in case types and outcomes

Impact of Prior History

Analysis of prior criminal history reveals:

  • Correlation between prior offenses and pretrial outcomes
  • Varying impact of different types of prior offenses
  • Implications for risk assessment

Data Quality

The dataset underwent thorough cleaning and preprocessing, including handling of missing values, standardization of categorical variables, and validation of temporal data. This ensures reliable analysis while maintaining transparency about data limitations and potential biases.
